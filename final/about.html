About the Process

What is a Tax Overage?

Tax foreclosure overages are the amount of funds remaining after a county sells a tax foreclosed property and recovers the taxes, penalties, and costs. If you are a prior owner of record of a tax foreclosed property that was subsequently sold, you may have surplus funds available to you. In the event that no claim for the surplus proceeds is received by the county after the property’s sale, the county claims the surplus funds. In many counties, it is not required that you are notified of the surplus funds available to you.

What We Do

Overages Claims Services US does extensive research to find properties that have been forclosed and which have surplus funds available to claim. We help you by explaining this process, preparing documentation, and helping claimants navigate deadlines and filing requirements in order to recieve those surplus funds.

After Filling

After the paperwork has been filled a submitted, Overages Claims Services US monitors the status of your claim. After the claim is approved the funds are released. We stay with you to assist until the funds are recieved.
